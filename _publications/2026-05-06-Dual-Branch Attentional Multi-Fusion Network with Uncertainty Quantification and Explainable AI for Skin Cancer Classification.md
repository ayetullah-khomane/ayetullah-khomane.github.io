Abstract: Skin cancer remains one of the most prevalent cancers worldwide, and early and accurate diagnosis is essential for reducing mortality. This study introduces AMF-Net, a novel dual-branch attentional multi-fusion network introducing a three-tier, uncertainty-based decision framework, directly addressing a critical gap in clinical readiness. Our unique framework integrates EfficientNet-B3 and ResNet-50 with enhanced CBAM attention and a new multi-component focal loss. The model was trained on a diverse 12,577-image dataset, including a newly collected hospital dataset, which significantly enhances clinical applicability beyond public benchmarks. Experimental results demonstrate state-of-the-art performance with 96.47% accuracy and ROC-AUC scores exceeding 0.90 across all seven lesion classes. Calibrated uncertainty quantification via Monte Carlo Dropout enables our three-tier system (automated, expert-assisted, expert-driven), mirroring real-world clinical workflows and ensuring patient safety. Grad-CAM++ visualizations further improve interpretability by highlighting clinically relevant features validated by dermatologists. An ablation study confirms the superiority of our dual-branch fusion design.
